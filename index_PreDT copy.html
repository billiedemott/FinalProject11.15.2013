↓ Take a starry Scroll through my life story. ↓
My path has felt a little like the movement of this copy: seemingly all over the place, but decoded*, has a discernable path.
I graduated Amherst College in 2007 with a piece of writing only an author (and thesis advisor) would love/read, and the desire to copywrite ads in the City (where I grew up). My way into the ad world was as a Broadcast Production P.A. at JWT.
After a year of PA-ing/project-managing, I knew I needed more. I turned to the liberal art student's default path: law school. As I worked to make this transition, I began working at a hedge fund.
Law school quickly fell off the table and for a moment, I thought finance was where I should be. But...
as the excitement of learning a new industry waned, I figured out that the hedge fund world was not where I ultimatley belonged. This dayjob would enable me to pursue (and to finance) side startup ventures that used my creative abilities and that I cared deeply about.
It is through the pursuit of these side experiences in the startup world that I've seen the direction I'd like my life to take.
My first real intro into the startup world was helping my friend market his virtual goods iPhone game, Tap Farm. The game was very successful, hitting the #1 free games spot, and eventually grew into a sucessful mobile gaming company, Pocket Gems. From reaching out to game reviewers to get press, to writing the game description, to looking into market competition, I did what I could to help my friends out. This experience formed my desire for my own thing.
Fast forward through a few other less serious ideas on the side...
My first real stab at building my own company has been founding Spoutlets. It is a mental health platform that lets individuals keep thought logs online. These logs can be shared with a therapist to enhance online and in-office therapy sessions, and can be shared with peers to get group support. Follow the elephant for more info...
My second company I co-founded is Workshop 902. WS902 lets designers/startup founders get constant feedback on their designs so they can rapidly iterate; it helps put into practice a lean, agile method of designing and developing. Further, WS brings the kind of discussion we host in our weekly UX Workshop at General Assembly online. Click the logo; learn more about the WS site and the weekly Workshop...
Me and my startup projects are still in development. I'm currently working at the hedge fund, but as I do this pay-gig, I am constantly expanding my skillset, my world, and in turn, increasing my happiness. (Admittedly, the number of hours I sleep is in shrinking mode.) Please contact me to hire me for UX, Front-End, or Copy-writing work, or to inquire about any of the above. You can also just say hi.
